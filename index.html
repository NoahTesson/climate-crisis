What are Greenhouse Gases?

Greenhouse gases are gases in Earth's atmosphere that trap heat. They allow sunlight to pass through the atmosphere, but prevent the heat that the sunlight brings from leaving the atmosphere. The main greenhouse gases include carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide, and various synthetic chemicals.

The Hottest Decade on Record

Due to rising greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions, the 2010s were the warmest decade in recorded history. According to NASA, 2020 tied with 2016 as the hottest year ever.

Arctic Amplification: Warming 4x Faster

Greenhouse gases drive Arctic amplification, where melting ice reduces surface reflectivity, causing more heat absorption. This feedback loop accelerates warming and ice loss.

Deforestation: A Major Carbon Contributor

Trees absorb CO2, but when forests are cleared, the stored carbon is released into the atmosphere. This worsens the greenhouse effect and reduces natural carbon sinks.

Water Vapor: The Overlooked Greenhouse Gas

While CO2 and methane get most of the attention, water vapor contributes the most to the greenhouse effect. However, it acts as a feedback gas, amplifying warming rather than driving it.

Fossil Fuels: The Top CO2 Culprit

The burning of coal, oil, and natural gas for energy and transportation is the largest source of human-caused CO2 emissions, significantly contributing to global warming.

Methane: Small but Mighty

Though CO2 is more abundant, methane traps 84 times more heat than CO2 over 20 years. Livestock, landfills, and oil and gas production are major methane sources.
